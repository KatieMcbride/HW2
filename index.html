Welcome to the Windy City!

Think Chicago is only deep-dish and hot dogs? Well, think again. Chicago has a food scene that rivals the biggest cities. While pizza and hot dogs may be our specialty, Chicago will surprise you with amazing food from Stephanie Izard's new Peruvian restaraunt, Cabra, to one of the top Japanese restaurants in America, Momotaro. This guide is short and sweet and includes my favorite places to eat. There are many places to go in Chicago and I hope this guide helps you narrow down your choices. I also include some tips on how to score a seat at the hottest restaurants.

Best Hamburger in Chicago: Au Cheval

While Au Cheval needs no explanation of why it is the best hamburger in Chicago, I'm still going to give it the recognition it deserves. Some say that it is the best burger in America, others say in the World. I heard the hype before going and afraid that it would not live up to all of the accolades and buzz. However, it lived up to every expectation and it truly was the best burger. Now here is the downside, the wait. If you go on a Friday or Saturday night be prepared to wait 4+ hours. The best time to go is when it opens and be one of the first inline. Or spend an evening in West Loop. Put your name down and then go enjoy the many bars and dessert shops around the neighborhood.

Chicago Classics

Chicago has been perfecting the hot dog, Italian beef, and deep dish pizza for a very long time. Italian beef sandwiches were created in Chicago in the 1930's. So be sure to go to Al's Beef to see where it all started. When it comes to pizza you will get a lot of divisive opinions of where the best pizza is. If you can, try them all. And lastly, an insider tip on hot dogs: do NOT put ketchup on it.
